Position Summary

The Project Manager is a core field delivery role within AFL's hyperscale PMO, responsible for independently managing the day-to-day execution of assigned fiber infrastructure projects within a hyperscale data center program. Reporting to the Senior Project Manager or Program Manager, this role owns the scope, schedule, cost, risk, quality, and safety of individual project assignments — typically covering defined workstreams of bulk fiber splicing, ISP/OSP cable installation, OTDR testing, and structured cabling within an active data center campus environment. The Project Manager coordinates directly with field crews, subcontractors, and client site representatives to keep work moving, tracks delivery progress against the project plan, manages day-to-day issues and reporting, and escalates risks and decisions beyond their authority to the Senior Project Manager. This role is the primary engine of AFL's field delivery and a key development pathway toward the Senior Project Manager level.

Key Responsibilities
Project Planning & Execution
  • Manage day-to-day delivery of assigned fiber infrastructure project workstreams within AFL's hyperscale data center programs — ensuring all activities are executed on scope, on schedule, within budget, and to AFL's quality and safety standards
  • Develop and maintain detailed project plans for assigned workstreams including task schedules, resource assignments, material staging requirements, and look-ahead plans aligned to the overall program schedule maintained by the Senior Project Manager
  • Participate actively in project kickoff meetings, daily field coordination huddles, weekly progress reviews, and milestone gate reviews — producing accurate field reports, meeting minutes, and action logs for distribution to the project team
  • Track daily and weekly production progress against the approved project schedule, identifying deviations from plan early and coordinating corrective actions with the Senior Project Manager
  • Maintain the project schedule in MS Project or Oracle Primavera P6 — updating actuals, revising look-ahead plans, and flagging schedule risks or critical path threats to the Senior Project Manager promptly
  • Support pre-construction planning activities including site walks, material take-offs, tool and equipment requisitions, and subcontractor mobilization coordination for upcoming project assignments
Field Coordination & Technical Oversight
  • Coordinate day-to-day field activities for assigned project workstreams — directing AFL self-perform crews and subcontractor teams on fiber installation sequencing, splicing activities, testing schedules, and site safety compliance
  • Apply solid working knowledge of fiber optic infrastructure — including fusion splicing, OSP aerial and underground cable installation, ISP fiber pulling and termination, OTDR testing, and optical loss measurement — to support field crews and resolve routine technical issues on site
  • Read, interpret, and apply construction drawings, splice diagrams, fiber routing plans, and engineering packages in the field — coordinating with the Senior Project Manager and engineering team when discrepancies or design clarifications are needed
  • Monitor subcontractor and crew productivity against daily and weekly production targets, documenting performance and escalating underperformance or quality issues to the Senior Project Manager
  • Coordinate installation sequencing with the broader data center campus construction schedule — liaising with general contractor site supervisors and other trades to maintain clear site access and avoid schedule conflicts
  • Track material consumption, equipment usage, and on-site inventory against the approved project budget, flagging shortfalls or procurement delays to the Senior Project Manager and AFL's procurement team
Schedule, Cost & Risk Management
  • Track project expenditures against the approved cost baseline for assigned workstreams — monitoring labor hours, material usage, and subcontractor costs daily and reporting cost status and variances to the Senior Project Manager
  • Maintain a current project risk log for assigned workstreams — identifying, documenting, and escalating risks to scope, schedule, cost, quality, and safety in a timely manner with proposed mitigation actions
  • Support the Senior Project Manager in processing project change orders — documenting scope changes, gathering field cost data, and preparing change order backup packages for review and submission
  • Review and validate subcontractor daily field reports, timesheets, and progress claims for accuracy and completeness before forwarding to the Senior Project Manager for approval
  • Assist in the preparation of earned value data and project cost forecasts at the workstream level to support program-level financial reporting
Stakeholder & Vendor Communication
  • Serve as the primary daily point of contact for client site representatives, general contractor site supervisors, and third-party vendors at the field level — communicating project status, field issues, and upcoming work activities clearly and professionally
  • Coordinate with AFL's procurement and logistics teams to ensure all fiber cable, splice hardware, connectors, conduit materials, testing equipment, and consumables are ordered, received, staged, and accounted for on site throughout the project
  • Communicate daily field progress, outstanding risks, and upcoming coordination needs to the Senior Project Manager through structured daily and weekly reporting — using AFL's standard reporting templates and communication protocols
  • Facilitate and document subcontractor coordination meetings, daily toolbox talks, and client field walkthroughs, ensuring all parties are aligned on upcoming work activities, access requirements, and safety expectations
  • Coordinate with AFL's QA/QC team to schedule fiber testing witness sessions, inspection hold points, and client acceptance walkthroughs at appropriate project milestones
Quality Assurance & Safety
  • Implement AFL's quality management plan on assigned workstreams — conducting daily installation inspections, monitoring splice quality, and verifying test results to ensure all fiber work meets TIA/EIA, BICSI, NEC, and client-specified acceptance standards
  • Review OTDR traces, optical insertion loss results, and splice attenuation records for accuracy and compliance with project acceptance criteria before submitting to the Senior Project Manager for client acceptance processing
  • Maintain accurate and complete project documentation throughout the delivery lifecycle — including daily field reports, RFI logs, material delivery records, quality inspection logs, splice records, test documentation, and as-built mark-ups
  • Enforce OSHA regulations, AFL's safety requirements, and client site safety protocols for all AFL and subcontractor personnel on site — leading daily toolbox talks, conducting job hazard analyses (JHAs), and completing regular field safety observations
  • Report all on-site safety incidents, near misses, and quality non-conformances to the Senior Project Manager immediately, supporting the investigation and corrective action process in accordance with AFL's incident reporting procedures
  • Support the project punch list process — tracking open quality and completion items, coordinating remediation work with field crews, and confirming sign-off with the client before workstream closeout
Reporting, Documentation & Closeout
  • Produce accurate, structured, and timely daily field reports, weekly progress updates, and schedule look-ahead plans using AFL's standard reporting tools and PMO templates — submitting all reports to the Senior Project Manager on the agreed reporting cadence
  • Maintain complete and well-organized project documentation for assigned workstreams throughout the delivery lifecycle, ensuring all records are current, accurate, and readily accessible for review by the Senior Project Manager, client, or QA team
  • Support the formal project closeout process — collating as-built drawings, OTDR test records, splice documentation, inspection sign-off sheets, and client acceptance records into a complete turnover package for Senior Project Manager review and formal client submission
  • Contribute lessons learned and delivery observations at the end of each project assignment, supporting AFL's continuous improvement initiatives and helping to refine field delivery playbooks and quality benchmarks for future hyperscale programs
Qualifications & Requirements
  • Bachelor's degree in Electrical Engineering, Telecommunications Engineering, or Civil/Construction Engineering required; Construction Management accepted
  • 4+ years of project management experience in fiber optic infrastructure, telecom construction, or data center environments, with demonstrated ability to manage project workstreams independently
  • 2+ years of direct field experience on OSP/ISP fiber projects or hyperscale data center construction sites
  • PMP certification required or actively pursuing; OSHA 30-Hour certification required
  • Solid working knowledge of fiber optic infrastructure including fusion splicing, OSP/ISP installation methods, OTDR testing, optical loss analysis, and splice documentation practices
  • Ability to read, interpret, and apply fiber construction drawings, splice diagrams, and engineering packages in an active field environment
  • Experience coordinating subcontractor crews and field teams, tracking productivity, and enforcing scope, quality, and safety standards on site
  • Working proficiency in project scheduling using MS Project or Oracle Primavera P6; ability to maintain look-ahead schedules and track actuals against baseline
  • Basic financial management skills including cost tracking, labor and material monitoring, and budget variance identification at the workstream level
  • Working knowledge of TIA/EIA, BICSI, NEC, NESC standards and OSHA safety regulations applicable to fiber and data center construction environments
  • Strong organizational skills with the ability to manage multiple concurrent field tasks, maintain accurate documentation, and report clearly to the Senior Project Manager
  • Willingness to work full-time on hyperscale data center campus sites with travel as required by project assignments; valid driver's license required
